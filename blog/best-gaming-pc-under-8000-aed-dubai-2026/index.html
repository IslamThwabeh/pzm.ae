Best gaming PC under 8,000 AED in Dubai 2026: real 1440p ultra build

1) Who this build is for

You want true 1440p ultra at 100+ FPS in modern AAA games, ray tracing on in supported titles, and headroom for AAA releases through 2027. This is also a great content creation box for streaming and 1080p/4K editing.

2) The parts list (~7,800 AED)

CPU: Ryzen 7 9700X or 7700X (~1,150). Motherboard: B650E or X670 ATX (~850). RAM: 32 GB DDR5 6000 CL30 (~580). GPU: RTX 5070 Ti or RX 9070 XT (~3,400). SSD: 2 TB Gen4 NVMe (~520). PSU: 850 W 80+ Gold (~480). Case: airflow ATX (~360). Cooler: 240 mm AIO or premium air (~440). Total: ~7,780 AED.

3) Where the budget goes

Roughly 44% of the build is GPU. That is correct — at 1440p ultra, the GPU sets your ceiling. Do not be tempted to spend less on GPU to add RGB, faster RAM, or Gen5 SSD; you will regret it within 6 months.

4) Why not just buy an RTX 5080

RTX 5080 adds 1,800-2,200 AED for roughly 25% more performance at 1440p. At this resolution you do not see the full benefit. If you mainly play at 1440p, stay with the 5070 Ti / 9070 XT tier and put the savings into a better monitor.

5) The monitor decision

Pair this PC with a 27-32 inch 1440p OLED or QD-OLED panel at 165-240 Hz. Budget 1,800-2,800 AED. A bad monitor will hide what this build can do. We carry several options at the store.

6) Future upgrade path

AM5 motherboard means you can drop in Ryzen 9 9950X3D later without changing anything else. The 850 W PSU has headroom for next-gen GPUs. The case and cooling are sized for 2028-era hardware. You are not buying a 2026 PC — you are buying a 5-year platform.
