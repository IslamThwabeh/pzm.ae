iPhone Battery Health at 80%? Replace, Sell, or Keep — Dubai 2026 Guide

If you opened Settings → Battery → Battery Health and saw 80% (or the dreaded "Battery Service" message), you have three honest options: replace the battery, sell and upgrade, or keep using the phone with reduced runtime. Below is the AED math for each path in Dubai 2026 so you can pick without guessing.

1. What 80% actually means

Apple's threshold of 80% is roughly the point where you start losing real screen-on hours noticeably, and the phone may start throttling peak performance to prevent unexpected shutdowns. The phone still works fine — it just stops being a "full day" phone, especially in UAE summer heat.

2. Option A — Replace the battery (AED, original-quality cell)

  • iPhone 11 / 12 / 13 series: 180 - 280 AED
  • iPhone 14 series: 220 - 320 AED
  • iPhone 15 / 16 series: 280 - 420 AED

Choose this if: the phone is iPhone 13 or newer, screen and camera and Face ID all work, and you keep phones 2+ more years. Replacement at our Al Barsha workshop takes 45-90 minutes; battery health resets to 100% and the warning clears.

3. Option B — Sell and upgrade (AED, trade-in math)

Phones at 80% take a trade-in hit because the next buyer (or our workshop) will need to rebattery. Typical 2026 reductions:

  • Below 80% battery: -150 to -300 AED off the standard trade-in band.
  • "Battery Service" warning showing: -200 to -400 AED.
  • Non-original battery already installed: -100 to -300 AED additional.

See the full per-model trade-in ranges in our used iPhone trade-in price guide. Choose this if: the phone is iPhone 11 or 12 and another fault is appearing, or you were already planning to upgrade within 6 months. Pairs well with browsing new iPhone 17 options or certified used iPhones.

4. Option C — Keep using it (free, but)

Below 80%, you will see: shorter days, occasional unexpected shutdowns in heat or low temperatures, and slower peak performance after the next iOS update. Choose this only if: you charge twice a day anyway, you are happy with current runtime, or you are 2-3 months away from buying a new phone and do not want to spend on a battery you will discard.

5. The honest decision flow

  1. Phone is iPhone 13/14/15/16, otherwise perfect? Replace battery.
  2. Phone is iPhone 11 or 12, or has other faults? Sell, get trade-in value, upgrade.
  3. Upgrading within 60 days anyway? Keep it, charge twice a day, sell when ready.

6. Two warnings about non-original batteries

  • The "Important Battery Message — Unknown Part" warning never goes away on non-original cells, even after install.
  • Settings will not show 100% health, and Apple will not service the device for unrelated issues if a non-original battery is detected. Always ask which battery is being installed.
